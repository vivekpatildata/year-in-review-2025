THEIA YEAR IN REVIEW

In a year when the world's oceans grew more contested, more complex, and more consequential than ever, Theia delivered the clarity that matters most. Built on a foundation of evidence, not guesswork.

Over the past twelve months, SynMax's maritime intelligence platform processed over 130 million square kilometres of satellite imagery every single day, fusing optical, SAR, and AIS data into a single, unified intelligence picture, going beyond the limits of traditional tracking and reporting systems of the world's marine environment.

33M+ Vessel Detections since 2024
13M+ Vessels Identified & attributed
164K+ Dark Ship Detections invisible to AIS

We caught more than 59,000 instances of AIS spoofing, vessels deliberately manipulating their broadcast locations to evade oversight. We also tracked nearly 136,000 ship-to-ship transfer events through imagery alone, shining a light on activity that often takes place far from shore and far from scrutiny.

But the real story of this year isn't just about what we measured. It's about what that intelligence made possible: for the analysts, operators, and decision-makers who depend on knowing what's actually happening at sea, not what someone's transponder claims is happening.

Fusing over 10+ datasets to deliver real insights: from sanctions enforcement to maritime security, from counter-narcotics operations to protecting marine ecosystems. Theia didn't just monitor the maritime domain this year; it redefined what's possible in understanding it.

Guangzhou Shipyard, Longxue Island
NO AIS

On 25 Feb 2025, three Chinese-built Shuiqiao Class barges, commonly known as "jack-up" barges, departed Guangzhou Shipyard at Longxue Island. These specialized construction vessels, capable of elevating above water on extendable legs, traveled without broadcasting any AIS signal.

Beach Operations

Nansan Island, China
ARTIFICIAL DOCK

On 27 Feb 2025, Theia detected the barges approximately 35 km southeast of Zhanjiang. By 28 Feb 2025, all three were observed lining up near a beach on Nansan Island.

Bailey Bridge Deployment

The barges used Bailey bridges to link with one another and connect onto the beach. This modular military bridging system enabled rapid creation of a temporary dock structure. The maneuver was repeated on at least two separate occasions.

SAR imagery obtained on 13 Mar 2025 confirmed the barges remained positioned on the beach with Bailey bridges linked, forming an operational artificial dock.

The Full Picture

Every day, Theia ingests over 45 million images across PlanetScope, Sentinel-1, and Sentinel-2, covering more than 130 million square kilometres of ocean surface. That volume of data, fused with AIS and processed through SynMax's detection algorithms, is what makes the numbers above possible.

Since 2024, the platform has attributed over 11.3 million detections to 318,842 unique vessels, with 73,851 new attributions processed every single week. It has flagged 14,478 MMSI sharers, vessels cycling through borrowed or duplicated identities to avoid scrutiny. It has identified 130,686 military vessel movements, tracked 1,789 aircraft carrier transits, and logged 564 submarine detections across the world's oceans.

These are not estimates. They are confirmed, image-verified intelligence products delivered at a scale and speed that no traditional monitoring system can match. From the Persian Gulf to the South China Sea, from the coast of Venezuela to the waters off Northern Europe, Theia sees what others miss.
